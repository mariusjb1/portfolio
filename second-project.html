Warwick Rail

4th-year Masters Project

Client

ES410 Group Project

Date

May 2020

Category

Group Project

An auto-stop system was designed and manufactured for the first time in order to compete in the auto-stop challenge for the IMechE Railway Challenge. Initially various sensor technologies were researched and tested including the Ultrasonic sensor recommended by the previous year. Infrared (IR) receivers and LEDs performed best, providing good reliability, affordability, robust detection, and were unaffected by rail track conditions as well as being easy to interface with. A prototype system was produced using an Arduino and breadboard so experimentation could be completed to determine the ideal number of IR LEDs and receiver that will work in the range desired. Veroboard was selected for mounting the Arduino and electronic components to deal with locomotive vibration as components are soldered in place.
